Marco Calvo
Password Cracker

An OSINT wordlist forge. Turn what you know about a target into a surgical dictionary - block by block, in the order you choose - then throw it at the hash with hashcat or john.

Breaking the mold

Online "personalized wordlist generators" hand you a fixed recipe: same template, same order, same predictable output. mcpscrk throws that away. Here you assemble the dictionary by hand, exactly how a real operator would attack this person.

No blind cartesian product spraying billions of strings nobody types. You decide every block, every transform, every loop. The wordlist is a weapon you machined yourself.

It does one thing

Reconnaissance becomes raw material. Material becomes blocks. Blocks become a blueprint, and the order of the blocks is the nesting of the loops. One job - building the right list - done properly.

The forge is only as sharp as your intel. If the list does not break the hash, you do not know the target well enough yet. Go back to recon and machine a better build.

The discipline

Forge, strike, refine. If the dictionary misses, the build was wrong - not the engine. This is a craft of iteration: tighten the intel, re-order the blueprint, machine it again. Use only on targets you are authorized to test.

Target Profile

Lowercase only, no spaces, values separated by commas - e.g. alderson,sepiol. Capitalization and leet are applied later, per block.
